Rosa’s Pizza — Website Redesign for Improved Flow and Visual Appeal

Rosa’s Pizza’s website had outdated information, an unappealing design, and confusing navigation that made it difficult for customers to find menus, locations, and ordering options. This case study highlights my collaboration with Rosa’s Pizza to redesign their website, improve visual appeal, and streamline the user flow.

Overview

Timeline: In Progress
Role: UX/UI Designer — Research, Wireframing, Visual Design, Prototyping

Problem

The original website had outdated menus and contact information, inconsistent design elements, and a flow that confused users when trying to view menu items or place orders. Rosa’s Pizza wanted a more modern design that would make it easy for customers to navigate and increase online engagement.

Research & Analysis

  • Reviewed current website analytics to identify most-used pages and pain points.
  • Conducted competitor analysis of local pizza websites for design inspiration and usability benchmarks.
  • Gathered customer feedback on website difficulties and expectations.

User Flows

Created simplified flows for:

  • Browsing menu categories
  • Placing an order online
  • Finding store locations and contact information

Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes tested layout, menu structure, and navigation improvements before moving into the final UI design.

Final UI

Reflection

The redesign improved the website’s usability, updated all outdated information, and created a visually appealing interface that guides users efficiently to the information they need. This project taught me the importance of balancing brand personality with usability for small business websites.
