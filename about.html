ABOUT

I’m Natasha Narine, a first-generation student studying Computer Information Systems with an App Development concentration at Georgia State University. My interdisciplinary track allows me to combine a strong creative foundation with technical execution to solve real problems and bring ideas to life.

I’m passionate about taking ideas from concept to execution, whether it’s an app, campus event, or personal project. I find the most fulfillment in creating experiences that bring people together, and I thrive in environments where curiosity, adaptability, and thoughtful problem-solving drive meaningful results. Above all, I aim to make technology enjoyable and purposeful, blending precision with personality in every project I take on.

My Process

When approaching an ambiguous problem, this is the general outline I follow:



Envision Outcome

I start by defining what success looks like. When the problem no longer exists, what does that reality look like?


Map the Steps

I define the problem space, outline key tasks, and prioritize what comes first, treating each roadmap like a journey that turns early ambiguity into a clear, actionable path.


Make it Exist Now, Refine Later

I let ideas exist in a rough form before polishing them, even the absurd or uncertain ones. This allows ideas to grow instead of getting stuck in perfection.


Organize and Elevate

I transform unstructured ideas into focused, high-quality solutions that are visually cohesive and grounded in intent.


This approach is how nearly every project in my portfolio, including this website, came to life. It’s an ideology I plan to carry into every future project and role, helping me stay adaptable, intentional, and turn ambiguity into clear, impactful outcomes.
